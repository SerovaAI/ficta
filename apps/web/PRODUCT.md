# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers, security-conscious teams, and regulated organizations evaluating whether ficta's model-traffic redaction boundary fits their workflow. They arrive skeptical of broad AI-security claims and need to understand the mechanism quickly.

## Product Purpose

apps/web is the public marketing site at ficta.sh. Its job is to make ficta's local redact-and-restore mechanism legible, point users to the open-source package and docs, and invite relevant teams to start a conversation.

## Brand Personality

Precise, operationally honest, and calm. The site should feel technically credible without theatrical security cues or inflated promises.

## Anti-references

- Cybersecurity theater: hooded figures, fear copy, matrix-green interfaces, generic padlock imagery.
- Flashy AI-SaaS grammar: gradient text, glassmorphism, decorative orbs, hero metrics, and template card grids.
- Over-claiming: copy or visuals that imply guarantees beyond the documented threat model.

## Design Principles

- Show the mechanism instead of asserting safety.
- Keep the strongest claims scoped to exact-match registered values and covered request surfaces.
- Make limits visible enough to build trust, without turning the page into a disclaimer wall.
- Keep the OSS install path and source visibility easy to find.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives, readable contrast on the dark-only palette, and text or shape cues alongside color-coded redaction/restoration states.
